Interactive Design

Interaction design (ID) is a form of Human : Computer interaction and is defined as the structure and behavior of interactive systems
to create a better user experience. Some priciples of interaction design are discoverability, signifiers, and feedback. Discoverability
is important to ID as users wont want to waste time looking around icons they actually need, so to prevent this all icons should be labeled or
given a name. Signifiers are important becuase they must be present when an object has optional uses to confirm the action desired is what
is being done. Feedback is important to notify users that the desired or undesired action took place, in order to provide continous information
on their actions. Lastly, great interaction design restricts the user of every action possible, but instead shows whats important to the user at
any given time.

Page layouts, formating, and other changeable aspects of a web page should be crafted towards the expecected users and viewers of the page. Conventional
page layouts are left sided leaning in which most content and text will be on that side of the screen. While this is standard, interfaces need to be clean,
aesthetically pleasing, and clear on the point of the pages intentional uses. Great websites are able to build around the standards in the community and add
own flair needed to standout, however revolutionary one's can take on more creativity adding animations, bold color options, unique layouts, and newly created
interactions that are used by everyone else.
